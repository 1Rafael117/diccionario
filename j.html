JURY

n.
A number of persons appointed by a court to assist the attorneys in preventing law from degenerating into justice.
Against all law and evidence, The prisoner was acquitted.
The judge exclaimed: "Is common sense To jurors not permitted?"
The prisoner's counsel rose and bowed: "Your Honor, why this fury?
By law the judge is not allowed To sit upon the jury."
G.J.

JOY

n.
An emotion variously excited, but in its highest degree arising from the contemplation of grief in another.
